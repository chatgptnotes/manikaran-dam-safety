# Problem Statement: India's Dam & Irrigation Crisis

## 1. Aging Dam Infrastructure

India is the world's third-largest dam-building nation with 5,334 large dams and over 25,000 medium and small dams.

| Metric | Value | Implication |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Large dams > 50 years old | 1,137 | Exceeding original design life |
| Large dams > 100 years old | 209 | Urgently need rehabilitation or decommissioning |
| Dams with adequate SHM | <15% | Majority rely on manual visual inspection |
| Dam failures (2017-2024) | 36 reported incidents | Annamayya Dam (2021): 30+ deaths; Tiware Dam (2019): 23 deaths |
| Investigations citing "no real-time monitoring" | 90%+ | Failure detected only after physical breach |

**Regulatory imperative:** The Dam Safety Act, 2021 mandates every specified dam to have "adequate instrumentation for monitoring dam behavior" (Section 8), establish Dam Safety Units, and file surveillance reports with NDSA/SDSO. Non-compliance carries penalties including imprisonment. Most states lack technical capacity to comply.

## 2. Irrigation Inefficiency

India's irrigation potential created (IPC): 113 million hectares. Irrigation potential utilized (IPU): ~90 million hectares — a **gap of 23 million hectares**.

- **Conveyance losses of 35-45%** due to unlined canals, seepage, evaporation, unauthorized tapping
- **Tail-end deprivation:** head-reach farmers over-irrigate while tail-end farmers receive little or no water
- **No real-time flow measurement:** allocation based on outdated Warabandi schedules
- **Delayed project completion:** average Indian irrigation project takes 20-30 years (Gosikhurd: started 1984, still incomplete in 2026)

## 3. The Vidarbha Context

Vidarbha (eastern Maharashtra, 11 districts including Nagpur) is one of India's most water-stressed and agrarian-distress-prone regions:

- **Farmer suicides:** highest concentration in India, water scarcity as primary driver
- **Incomplete irrigation projects:** VIDC manages dozens where canals remain unbuilt decades after dam completion
- **Political sensitivity:** Gosikhurd debated in Parliament; PM approved INR 25,972 crore for completion
- **Institutional capacity gap:** VIDC (HQ: Nagpur) lacks digital monitoring capabilities

## 4. The Opportunity

The Dam Safety Act creates the same regulatory compulsion for dam operators that CERC's Deviation Settlement Mechanism created for RE generators. Dam operators need a "Water QCA" — an agency that ingests sensor data, runs AI models, and files compliance reports. No such service exists at scale in India today.
